About the Jon Peterson Special Needs Scholarship

The Jon Peterson Special Needs Scholarship, established by the State of Ohio in 2012, provides families of children with disabilities real educational choice. It enables parents to select and fund specialized private or alternative public programs—outside of their home district—that can deliver the services outlined in their child's Individualized Education Program (IEP).

Key Features of the Jon Peterson Scholarship

Eligibility

Children from kindergarten through age 21 (and up to age 22 if graduation requirements are unmet) with a finalized IEP generated from their public school district are eligible to apply.

Usage of Funds

Scholarship funds may be used to cover tuition and services at approved private or alternative public providers that implement the child's IEP—or additional services agreed upon by both the provider and family.

Funding Amount

Scholarships are awarded based on the student's primary disability category as identified in the Evaluation Team Report (ETR). Awards typically range from $9,500 to over $32,000 for the 2024–2025 year.

Capacity Cap

The program is capped such that scholarships cannot exceed 5% of the total number of Ohio students with disabilities. If demand exceeds availability, a lottery system may be used.

Application Process

Applications are continuous year-round. Families must select a participating provider, who then submits the application on their behalf.

Renewal

Scholarships must be renewed annually. As long as the student continues to be eligible under IDEA and maintains a current IEP, families may submit a renewal for the subsequent year.

How to Apply for the Jon Peterson Scholarship

Follow these 7 simple steps to get started with the Jon Peterson Scholarship application process:

1

Confirm Eligibility

  • Your child must have a current IEP (Individualized Education Program) from your public school district.
  • Your child must be an Ohio resident.
2

Choose a Provider

  • Select a state-approved Jon Peterson Provider (like The Reading Roost Educational Services).
  • Providers are licensed and registered with the Ohio Department of Education & Workforce (ODEW).
3

Notify Your School District

  • Tell your child's public school that you plan to use the Jon Peterson Scholarship.
  • The district will continue to manage and update your child's IEP.
4

Complete the Parent Forms

  • Your provider will give you the Parent Agreement Form and other documents.
  • Sign the forms so the provider can apply on your behalf.
5

Gather Required Documents

Provide the following to your provider:

  • Copy of your child's IEP
  • Proof of Ohio residency (utility bill, lease, etc.)
  • Copy of your child's birth certificate
  • Any additional forms your provider requests
6

Provider Submits the Application

  • Your provider submits everything online through the ODEW portal.
  • Parents do not submit the application themselves.
7

Approval & Services Begin

  • Once approved, your provider bills the state directly.
  • You do NOT pay out of pocket—services are fully covered by the scholarship.
Remember: The scholarship renews each year. Keep your IEP updated so your child can continue receiving services.

Free Educational Support for Students on IEPs

Learning Aid Ohio provides funding for in-person, supplemental learning support for students with IEPs. Families are matched with tutors who help students stay on track with their unique academic goals—based on their individual needs and school curriculum.

Eligibility Requirements:

  • Ohio residents
  • Students on an IEP
  • Household income under 400% of the Federal Poverty Level (e.g., $124,800 for a family of four)

How It Works:

  1. Apply online at learningaidohio.org
  2. Search for a tutor by expertise, grade, travel distance, and availability
  3. Schedule an introductory interview to ensure a great fit
